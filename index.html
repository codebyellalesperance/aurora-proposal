→ Human Delivers → Human Tracks

The Architecture

Five layers. One system.

01

Automated Intelligence Layer

Ingests member data. Surfaces signals, not just information.

Calendar parser → Detects patterns, gaps, conflicts
Health integration → Flags HRV drops, sleep issues
Intent extractor → Finds stated goals vs. reality
Signal generator → Outputs prioritized moments
Example output: "Ethan: HRV down 24pts, board meeting in 5 days → Recovery intervention"
Human role: Review signals. Add context. Override priorities.
Time saved: 45 min/member → 5 min/member
02

Recommendation Engine

Matches signals to pre-vetted inventory. Generates 80% drafts.

Inventory database → Tagged by location, vibe, category
Matching algorithm → Connects signals to options
Template library → Frameworks for common rec types
Draft generator → Outputs near-complete recs
Human role: Refine the draft. Add insight. Adjust tone.
Time saved: 30 min/rec → 5 min/rec
03

Timing & Delivery

Determines when to send. Manages channels. Prevents fatigue.

IF hrv < baseline * 0.8
AND next_event > 2 hours
AND last_rec > 24 hours
→ TRIGGER: recovery recommendation
→ CHANNEL: app notification
Human role: Set the rules. Override when context demands.
04

Execution & Fulfillment

This is Aurora's moat. Anyone can recommend. Aurora books.

Availability check → Real-time inventory
Booking automation → Direct integrations + concierge
Confirmation flow → Member confirms → booking executes
Fallback system → Alt suggestion if unavailable
Rule: Never recommend something you can't deliver.
05

Feedback & Optimization

Every interaction teaches the system.

Open rate Is timing right?
Confirmation rate Is it relevant?
Execution rate Did they do it?
Satisfaction Did it delight?

Learning loops:

Additional Considerations

The difference between proactive and intrusive is one message.

The Line Between Magic and Creepy

Personalization has a threshold. Cross it and care becomes surveillance.

Magic

"Your HRV is down—here's a recovery option."

Creepy

"You seem stressed about your board meeting."

Surface patterns. Don't narrate them. Let members connect their own dots.

When NOT to Recommend

Proactive doesn't mean constant.

Go quiet when:

  • Major life event (death, divorce, crisis) → Pause. "We're here when you need us."
  • Engagement drops suddenly → Don't increase volume. Reach out personally.
  • They're on a genuine retreat → Don't interrupt with more options.
  • 3+ declined recs in a row → Recalibrate. Ask what changed.

Sometimes presence without action is the most proactive move.

Cultural Calibration

Global members, local context.

  • Tokyo dining: the experience matters, not the Instagram following
  • LA dining: social proof matters more
  • Gifting: 4 is unlucky in Chinese culture; white flowers signal mourning in parts of Asia; alcohol doesn't work for everyone

Tag inventory with cultural considerations. Never assume culture from location.

The Trust Ladder

Not every member is ready for "we booked you a float tank."

1 Logistics "Your flight is confirmed."
2 Decisions "We recommend X over Y."
3 Anticipation "Your HRV is down—here's an option."
4 Architecture "Here's your year mapped out."
5 Delegation "Aurora, just handle it."

Never skip levels. Earn the right to go deeper.

Failure Recovery

Recommendations will fail. The response defines the brand.

Recommendation miss

"That wasn't right. Tell us what we missed." Update profile. Never repeat.

Execution failure

Own it. No excuses. Immediate alternative + something extra.

Timing failure

"Bad timing on our end. This will be here when you're ready."

Failures handled well build more trust than successes.

Privacy as Product

Intimate access is a privilege, not a right.

  • Minimize: Collect only what improves recommendations
  • Transparency: Members see what Aurora knows; "why this rec?" always has an answer
  • Control: Easy opt-out, pause button that works, different comfort levels for different areas

Aurora knows a lot so it can do a lot. Members control both.
